Living room in a tiny flat in Ufa. The room is empty but for an old sofa and a picture of a tree on the wall. The wallpaper is peeling off, the door is squeaky. Time: 23:56

[Albine is sitting on the sofa curled up, reading Hamlet. Iskander enters.]

ALBINE.

Hey, what are you doing here? And still not asleep? It is becoming unbearable, I can’t stand it when you’re not asleep so late!!! What a stupid son I have!

ISKANDER.

Everything worked out. Things are good. I’ve managed.

ALBINE.

What have you managed? To annoy me? Yes, you’ve always been remarkably good at driving me mad. Here you go!

ISKANDER. [in a raised manner]

It’s not necessary to throw your worthless Hamlets at me, I’ve done nothing to you, mum, you know!

[Sivilla enters the room, looks at Iskander, then at Albine, then at Hamlet on the floor. Picks up Hamlet.]

SIVILLA.

I totally agree with you, mum. Iskander is worthless. I’m definitely better a kid than he is. Here you go, Iskander.

ISKANDER.

And I have done nothing bad to you definitely, sis. And why do you throw Hamlet at me and not a slipper, as usual? Lost it? You always lose everything and you are dumb, so don’t underestimate yourself, you’re as hopeless as I am.

ALBINE.

Anyways, what have you managed? Definitely something that wasn’t even worth a struggle, was it?

ISKANDER.

Well, if you wish it to be this way, then it definitely is. I’ve closed the deadline. Solved an unsolvable task, you know. I was despairingly trying to work out the answers for six hours, and scored 91%, can you imagine? You should be elated by the fact I’m so brainy and able.

ALBINE. [with a smirk]

Yes, indeed, my son can’t even solve a task perfectly well and get a 100% grade, of course I should be happy, shouldn’t I?

SIVILLA.

Hah, yes, mum, he’s useless. Totally.

[Sivilla goes to the kitchen, returns. A rotten pear flies right in Iskander’s face.]
